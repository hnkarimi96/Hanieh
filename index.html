My Story

I am a Pharm.D graduate from Shahid Beheshti University of Medical Sciences in Tehran, Iran, specializing in Pharmaceutical Nanotechnology. With three years of research experience in this field, I have developed a deep understanding of novel drug delivery methods, including nanoparticles, hydrogels, nanofibrous scaffolds, and lipid-drug complexes. Throughout my academic journey, I have made significant contributions to the scientific community. I am proud to have published three papers in prestigious journals, showcasing my expertise in drug delivery systems. With my strong background in project management and exceptional teamwork skills, I am confident in my ability to excel in a Ph.D. program. I am dedicated to pushing the boundaries of drug delivery technology and contributing to the development of innovative solutions that can improve patient outcomes.

Research Interests

My research interests lie in the field of drug and gene delivery, specifically focusing on the utilization of nanoparticles, hydrogels, and nanofibers.

Lab Skills

I am experted in the preparation of liposomes (thin-film hydration), nanofibers (electrospinning), hydrogel, and drug-lipid complex, and I have experience in using imaging techniques (SEM, TEM, and AFM), FTIR, XRD, NMR, DSC, drug release, and HPTCL .

Health Educator

PakDasht, Tehran, Iran - 2017

In one of the most deprived areas of Tehran, Iran, people, including children, lack awareness of essential health information. As a group of pharmacy students, we traveled to this region and provided children with information on how to use a toothbrush and how to identify if they have lice. Additionally, we measured the blood pressure and blood sugar levels of adults in this area, informing them about their health conditions. This experience has been truly inspiring in my student life!

Educational Game Designer

PersisGen Pharmaceutical Company - 2019

Games can be highly effective tools for learning, as they enhance the learning process by making it more interactive and enjoyable. During an event organized by PersisGen Company, two of my classmates and I had the opportunity to design an educational game aimed at teaching pharmacy students the fundamental principles of pharmaceutical sciences, pharmacology, and drug chemistry. Numerous student groups participated in the game, attempting to solve riddles before the outbreak of a dreadful disease in the city! This innovative approach successfully engaged and challenged the students, providing them with a creative learning experience.
